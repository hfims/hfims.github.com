Activity 1--Smokebomb

Sep 19th, 2012

中文版

This is the whole process of making a simple smokebomb.

Materials:

KNO3, normal sugar, Karo syrup, tap water, paper box

Tools:

Pot, gas stove, salt, glass bowl, stirring rod, asbestos-free wire gauze, lighter, 50mL volumetric cylinder, balance, hand, gloves, iron wire, tongs.

Procedures:

  1. Use the balance to measure 60 grams of KNO3 and 40 grams of sugar and put them into the glass bowl
  2. Add 30mL of water
  3. Put a layer of salt on the pot
  4. Put the bowl on the salt
  5. Put the pot on the stove, use the smallest fire to heat and use the stirring rod to stir
  6. When boiled, put about 1mL of syrup into the bowl
  7. If there are lots of bubbles, put out the fire and continue to stir. When bubbles stop producing, start the fire again
  8. About 40 minutes later, there are lots of solid, speed up the stirring
  9. When the surface of solid look smooth, start testing: put out the fire, continue to stir, and use tongs to take a little piece of solid and wait for it to cool down. If the solid can be cracked, then the fuel is done.
  10. When the solid in the bowl is cooler, use hands gloves to remove the solid and place into the paper box
  11. Wait until fully cooled down.
  12. Done!

This is using recrystalization, so the quality of fuel is VERY good. If use the lighter to heat the iron wire and poke the wire into the piece of fuel used to test, it burns quickly and releases lots of smoke and remains nothing. Great effects!
